The science

What exosomes actually do to your scalp

Not another serum claim — the real biology behind why this works.

Exosomes are tiny vesicles released by stem cells — think of them as messengers, not building blocks. They don't replace anything in your scalp. Instead, they carry instructions: growth factors, proteins, and signaling molecules that tell your existing follicles to wake up, repair, and keep growing.

Why your scalp stops growing hair in the first place

Hair thinning rarely happens because follicles disappear. More often, they go dormant — pushed into a resting phase by chronic inflammation, poor circulation, or stress on the scalp. The follicle is still there. It's just not getting the signal to do its job.

This is the gap exosome therapy is built to close. Rather than masking the problem with styling or coverage, it works at the level where the problem actually starts.

"Exosomes don't grow hair on their own — they remind your follicles how."

The two things exosomes actually do

Mechanism 01

Calm the inflammation

Chronic, low-grade inflammation around the follicle is one of the most common — and most overlooked — causes of thinning. Exosomes carry anti-inflammatory signals that reduce this irritation, protecting follicles from the slow damage that inflammation causes over time.

Mechanism 02

Restore circulation

Healthy hair growth depends on a steady supply of oxygen and nutrients. Exosomes support the formation of new blood flow pathways to the scalp, improving delivery to areas that have been undernourished — often for years.

What this looks like during a Replenish session

At MOR.e, exosome therapy is delivered as part of Program 03, Replenish. One vial is applied directly to the scalp following a full head spa ritual and therapeutic massage — the massage itself helps circulation carry the exosomes further, and LED light afterward supports absorption and calms the scalp post-treatment.

Most guests describe the session as deeply relaxing rather than clinical — there's no injection, no downtime, and no need to plan your week around it.

Common questions

Is this the same as PRP?

No. PRP uses your own blood platelets, which requires a blood draw. Exosomes are lab-derived and don't require any blood collection — the session itself is needle-free and far gentler.

How many sessions will I actually need?

Most guests see meaningful change with a series of 2–3 sessions, spaced four to six weeks apart, followed by occasional maintenance every few months. Because we pair this with cold plasma preparation, many guests notice initial change sooner than this timeline suggests. We'll map out a realistic plan together at your first visit.

Does it hurt?

No. There's no needle involved in our protocol — the scalp is left comfortable, sometimes slightly warm from the LED step, and that's it.

The innovation

Cold plasma — ionized water, made for skin

Patented technology that sterilizes, rebuilds collagen, and supports elastin.

Cold plasma sounds futuristic because it is — but the idea behind it is simple. By physically ionizing water, we create a solution that does two things skin care rarely manages to do at the same time: sterilize gently and stimulate repair.

c

Patented technology. Our cold plasma water is developed under a registered patent for cold plasma beauty device manufacturing — not an off-the-shelf ingredient, but a process we've built and applied directly into our own product line.

What "ionized plasma water" actually means

Plasma is sometimes called the fourth state of matter — alongside solid, liquid, and gas. Cold plasma technology uses a physical (not chemical) process to ionize water, changing its molecular activity without heat or harsh additives. The result is a water-based solution that's biologically active in a way ordinary water isn't.

Because the process is physical rather than chemical, it avoids the kind of irritation that strong actives can cause — which is part of why it pairs so well with sensitive, post-treatment scalp skin.

Two things happening at once

Effect 01

Sterilizes with reactive oxygen

The reactive oxygen species generated by the ionization process help cleanse the skin's surface — supporting a calmer, less reactive scalp environment before any other treatment is applied.

Effect 02

Stimulates collagen production

The same activity that sterilizes also signals fibroblasts to increase collagen synthesis — the structural protein that keeps skin (and the scalp's surface) firm and well-supported.

Effect 03

Increases elastin generation

Alongside collagen, elastin production rises — supporting the skin's ability to bounce back, which matters as much on the scalp as it does anywhere else on the body.

Why this matters for your scalp specifically

A scalp under chronic stress often shows the same signs as aging skin elsewhere — reduced elasticity, a compromised surface barrier, and a slower recovery cycle. By sterilizing and stimulating structural protein production in the same step, cold plasma water gives the scalp's surface a better foundation to support the deeper work being done by massage and exosome therapy.

The part no other clinic in Canada offers

On its own, cold plasma prepares and calms the surface. On its own, exosome therapy delivers growth factors to the follicle. Used together — which is the protocol unique to MOR.e — the calmed, sterilized surface lets exosomes absorb faster and more completely, while the anti-inflammatory effect continues working underneath. That combination is what makes results arrive sooner, and what makes the protocol gentle enough for scalps that other treatments would irritate.

Is this safe for sensitive skin?

Yes — and this is actually where the combination shines most. No — they're different technologies that complement each other. LED uses light wavelengths to support absorption and calming; cold plasma uses ionized water to sterilize and stimulate collagen and elastin production at the surface level, ahead of exosome application.
